Key Events in Singapore, 1942–1945

Dec 8, 1941

First Bombing of Singapore

Japanese forces bomb Singapore just hours after attacking Pearl Harbor, signaling the start of the Malayan Campaign.

Feb 13–15, 1942

Battle of Bukit Chandu

The Malay Regiment makes its last stand at Opium Hill, delaying Japanese troops and becoming a symbol of resistance.

Feb 15, 1942

British Surrender

British forces formally surrender at the Old Ford Factory, marking the beginning of the Japanese Occupation of Singapore.

1943

Rationing and Black Markets

Severe shortages force civilians to rely on ration cards and secret black markets, reshaping daily survival.

Sep 1945

End of Occupation

Japanese forces surrender after the atomic bombings in Japan. British return to Singapore and begin the process of rebuilding.
